Ir. Soekarno

The first President of Indonesia

Here's a time line of Ir. Soekarno's life:

  • 1901 - Born in Soerabaja
  • 1926 - Soekarno obtained an Ingenieur degree in study civil engineering (with a focus on architecture) at the Technische Hoogeschool te Bandoeng (Bandoeng Institute of Technology)
  • 1927 - Soekarno with his friends from the Algemeene Studieclub established a pro-independence party, the Indonesian National Party (PNI), of which Soekarno was elected the first leader.
  • 1930 - Soekarno was sentenced to four years in prison, which were served in Sukamiskin prison in Bandung. His speech, however, received extensive coverage by the press, and due to strong pressure from the liberal elements in both Netherlands and Dutch East Indies
  • 1943 - Soekarno and Hatta were sent on a 17-day tour of Japan, where they were decorated by Emperor Hirohito and wined and dined in the house of Prime Minister Hideki Tojo in Tokyo.
  • 1945 - Soekarno returned to his house at Jalan Pegangsaan Timur No. 56, where Mohammad Hatta joined him. Throughout the morning, impromptu leaflets printed by PETA and youth elements informed the population of the impending proclamation. Finally, at 10 am, Soekarno and Hatta stepped to the front porch, where Soekarno declared the independence of the Republic of Indonesia in front of a crowd of 500 people. This most historic of buildings had, however, been ordered to be demolished by Soekarno himself, without any apparent reason.
  • 1954 - Soekarno married Hartini, a 30-year-old widow from Salatiga, whom he met during a reception.
  • 1960 - Soekarno began an aggressive foreign policy to secure Indonesian territorial claims. In August of that year, Soekarno broke off diplomatic relations with the Netherlands over the continuing failure to commence talks on the future of Netherlands New Guinea, as was agreed at the Dutch-Indonesian Round Table Conference of 1949.
  • 1967 - Soekarno's "Complementary Nawaskara" speech was also delivered on January 10, 1967 but was later rejected by the MPRS on February 16 of the same year. Until finally on February 20, 1967 Soekarno signed the Statement of Transfer of Power at the Merdeka Palace.
